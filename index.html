Sebastian Bugge Loeschcke

I am a Ph.D. student at the Computer Science department at the IT University of Copenhagen and part of the Pioneer Centre for Artificial Intelligence, advised by Michael J. Kastoryano and Serge Belongie. Additionally, I am honored to be a Danish Data Science Academy (DDSA) fellow. My research interests are in Machine Learning and Computer Vision with a special focus on using Tensor Network methods for effecient Image Rendering.


I hold B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees from Aarhus University, specializing in Machine Learning. I have also worked as a Computer Vision and Machine Learning engineer at AMCS Group and in a Data Science role at Systematic.

Progressive Parameter Space Visualization for Task-Driven SAX Configuration
Sebastian Loeschcke, Marius Hogräfer, Hans-Jörg Schulz

EuroVA, 2020. (Oral Presentation)

  Paper

We propose a task-driven parametrization of PAA and SAX through a parameter space visualization that shows the difference of progressively running a given analytic computation on the original and on the reduced data for a representative set of data samples.
